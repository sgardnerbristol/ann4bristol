I have been an active and diversified volunteer in Bristol

  • Property Taxes
    • 2008 - 2012 - When the property values in Bristol skyrocketed and we had issues with the re-valuation company that Bristol hired so I got involved. I learned how the process worked and fought hard to resolve issues that resulted. In 2011, I was appointed to a ‘Property Revaluation Advisory Committee’ with the intent on developing guidelines for the conduct of future reappraisals. This committee generated a report and made several suggestions to increase transparency to the process as well as reduce errors and the need for appeals. Just last year I was appointed to a Revaluation Ad Hoc Committee tasked with pre-reviewing all of assessment data before it was finalized and mailed to Bristol residents. Errors were identified and corrected. According to our tax assessor, Chris Belair, this reduced the number of appeals significantly.
  • Community Outreach
    • 2009 – 2015 – Participated in the Bristol Elks “Turkey Roll” event for 7 years and chaired the event for two years. Activities involve advertising, solicitation of raffle items from supporting vendors, event planning, ticket sales, hosting event, and filling / delivery of baskets. This fundraising event took months to plan and resulted in proceeds in excess of $25,000 spent directly on food baskets and supporting local food pantries for the Thanksgiving holiday. This made an impact on over 600 families in the area.
  • Leadership
    • 2008 - Present - President, Bristol Highlands Improvement Association (BHIA). In charge of leading the not-for- profit organization with regards to annual budgets, maintenance of properties and buildings, as well as conducting the 100th (2011) year celebration for the organization. Assets for BHIA are in excess of $2.5 million dollars. Prior activities include Board Member at large & Vice President. Current position is Secretary.
  • Education
    • 2005 – 2015 - Participated on the Roger Williams University Engineering Professional Advisory Board. The primary duties were to ensure that the engineering students were being afforded with the best curriculum possible to prepare them for the future careers.
  • Project Management
    • As a long-time employee for Raytheon I have had numerous leadership opportunities where I was responsible for project management, including financial planning, monitoring, and reporting. I was also responsible for career development of the employees that worked for me. Budgets managed have been in excess of $10M.
